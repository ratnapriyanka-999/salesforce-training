 # Day 1 - CRM Basics

## What is CRM?
CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is a system used by companies to manage customer information, interactions, sales, and support services in an organized way.

CRM helps businesses improve customer relationships, track communication, and manage business workflows more efficiently.
## Why Companies Use Salesforce
Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform used by companies to manage customers, sales, marketing, and support activities in one place.

Companies use Salesforce because it:
- Helps organize customer data
- Improves communication with customers
- Automates business processes
- Tracks sales and opportunities
- Improves teamwork and productivity
## Important Salesforce Concepts

### Account
An Account represents a company or organization associated with the business.

Example:
- A college
- A hospital
- A company
### Contact
A Contact represents a person related to an Account.

Example:
- Student
- Patient
- Employee
- Customer
### Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a potential business deal or sales process.

It helps companies track:
- Deal progress
- Revenue
- Sales stages
- Expected closing date
## Business Workflow Understanding

### Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

- A Lead is a person who shows interest in a product or service.
- After qualification, the Lead becomes a Contact.
- A business deal is then tracked as an Opportunity.
- Once the deal is successful, the person becomes a Customer.
