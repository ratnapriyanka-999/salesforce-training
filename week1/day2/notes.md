# Day 2 - Leads and Opportunities in Salesforce
## What I learned today
Today I learned about Leads and Opportunities in Salesforce and how they are connected to the sales process. I also explored features like Opportunity Teams, Path, and Kanban view, which help in managing sales activities more efficiently.
## What is a Lead?
A Lead is a person or organization that might be interested in a company’s product or service. Leads can come from different sources like websites, campaigns, events, or referrals.
### Key points about Leads
- Leads represent potential customers  
- They store basic customer information  
- Leads can later be converted into Accounts, Contacts, and Opportunities  
## What is an Opportunity?
An Opportunity represents a potential sales deal with a customer. It helps companies track the progress of deals, expected revenue, and different stages in the sales cycle.
### Key points about Opportunities
- Opportunities are used to manage sales deals  
- They pass through different stages before closing  
- Revenue and close dates can be tracked easily  
## Difference between Lead and Opportunity

| Lead | Opportunity |
|------|-------------|
| Potential customer | Potential sales deal |
| Beginning stage | Sales process stage |
| Can be converted later | Used to track deal progress |
## Opportunity Teams
I learned that Opportunity Teams allow multiple users to work together on the same opportunity. Team members can be assigned different roles and permissions based on their responsibilities.
## Opportunity Splits
Today I also understood different types of opportunity splits:
- **Revenue Split** → given to members directly responsible for the revenue  
- **Overlay Split** → given to supporting members who helped indirectly  
## Path and Kanban View
### Path
Path helps users follow different stages in the sales process. It also provides guidance and makes tracking progress easier.
### Kanban View
Kanban view displays records in a visual column format. It helps users manage opportunities more easily by moving records between stages using drag and drop.
## My understanding
Today’s topics helped me understand how Salesforce manages the journey from identifying potential customers to tracking sales opportunities and closing deals. Features like Path and Kanban make the process more organized and user-friendly.
